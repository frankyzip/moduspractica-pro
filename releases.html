Release Notes
What's new in ModusPractica Pro
Every update explained in plain language — what changed, why it changed, and what it means for your practice.
Version 1.1
June 2026
Includes versions 1.1.0 – 1.2.0
Update

A series of improvements across the entire app — more control over your repertoire, a clearer view of your practice calendar, time tracking for sight reading and warm-ups, and a more robust and secure foundation under the hood.

Latest — version 1.2.0
🎧 Reference MP3 per piece

Attach a local MP3 from your hard drive as reference audio for any piece — for example a recording, backing track, or study version. Click the 🎧 button in the piece detail view to choose a file; the button turns blue when audio is saved. An inline player lets you listen while you work on the piece. Replace or remove the file anytime from the same menu. MP3s are stored locally in your browser and are included in data backups. External web links and YouTube URLs remain on the separate link button.

Version 1.1.9
🔗 Links on pieces

Attach a URL to any piece — IMSLP, YouTube, or any other web link. The address is never shown as text; a link icon in your repertoire list and piece detail opens it in a new tab. Add a link to an existing piece anytime, or replace it when you find a better source. The icon turns blue when a link is set, grey when none is saved.

Versions 1.1.5 – 1.1.8
🔍 Analysis limited to the initial phase

The Analysis button on a section practice page is now available only during the Exploration phase (initialDaysDone < 3). Once a section enters Consolidation, the training counter is the correct way to register attempts — this protects your scheduling data from hidden rehearsal during analysis. The standalone 🎹 Free Practice button in the navigation bar is unchanged. Switching from analysis to training on the same day shows a short reminder about cold-start accuracy.

📊 Clearer Practice Time chart

The Statistics bar chart now shows three stacked segments per day: training (blue), analysis (orange), and Free Practice (green). Hover a bar to see the day total first, then the breakdown — Free Practice is listed only when you logged time that day. Totals no longer look inconsistent when Free Practice was part of the session.

Versions 1.1.5 – 1.1.7
📅 Schedule month view

The Schedule page now has a Month tab alongside the existing list view. A calendar grid shows which days you practised (green, intensity by duration) and which sections are due today or ahead. Click any day for session detail and due sections. Overdue items are grouped on today.

📊 Time tracking only

Some practice does not belong in your spaced repetition schedule — sight reading, warm-ups, exploring new material. Enable Time tracking only on a piece in the piece detail view. Sessions log practice time for Statistics but skip the training counter and FSRS planning. Ideal for sight-reading books or daily warm-up routines.

🎹 Smarter repertoire dates

Pieces marked for time tracking only now show the correct last-practiced date in your repertoire list, based on session history rather than the scheduling fields used for structured training.

Earlier in version 1.1
🗑️ Direct delete for sections

You can now permanently delete a section directly from the piece detail view — no need to archive it first and hunt for it in the archive. Both active and archived sections get a delete button right where you see them. Practice history is always preserved in Statistics, regardless of whether a section is deleted.

🎹 Free Practice mode

A new Free Practice button appears in the navigation bar on every page. It opens a practice session without linking it to any section or piece — useful for improvisation, warm-ups, or exploring new material. Free Practice time appears separately in your Statistics so it never inflates your structured training numbers.

🛡️ Smarter FrustrationGuard

The FrustrationGuard now counts your total error burden — failed attempts and streak resets combined. In Strict Gebrian mode, each reset already adds repetitions to your target; the guard now catches that correctly. Warning at 5 total errors, hard stop at 8.

🔬 Expanded scientific foundation

A new Science page documents the research behind every algorithm and design decision in the app. Three additional peer-reviewed references added. The tempo-progression rationale now clearly distinguishes between what the research says and what the app implements as a design choice.

🔒 Security and stability hardening

Backup import is now atomic — if anything goes wrong during a restore, your existing data rolls back automatically. User-entered text is sanitised before display. The scheduling engine now recovers gracefully from corrupt or missing values instead of writing invalid dates.

Version 1.0.0
June 6, 2026
First public release

ModusPractica Pro is officially here. This is the first public release — a complete practice planner built from the ground up on real motor learning science. Everything has been designed around one question: what does your brain actually need to turn repeated practice into lasting skill?

🧠 Spaced repetition scheduling

The app calculates when each section of your repertoire needs your attention — not too soon, not too late. Based on the FSRS algorithm, the same science used in the best language learning tools, now applied to motor skills.

🎵 Automatic tempo progression

When you consistently nail a section, the app nudges your practice tempo upward. Small, proportional steps — because research shows sudden large jumps in tempo undo consolidation.

🛡️ FrustrationGuard

When a session goes wrong and frustration builds, the app stops you. Not because you failed — but because practicing while frustrated encodes the errors, not the corrections. A fresh attempt after sleep is always more effective.

⚡ Entry Cost

A metric that tells you more than success rate ever could: how many errors do you make before your first correct repetition? When that number drops, your motor memory is genuinely consolidating.

🤖 Groq AI analysis

Optional. Add your own Groq API key and the app generates a plain-language analysis of your practice patterns — what's progressing, what's stalling, and what to focus on next.

🔒 Your data, your device

Everything stays in your browser. No account, no server, no cloud. Export a backup anytime and import it into any future version of the app — nothing is ever lost.
